How to Support Your Child

Encourage Open Communication

  • Ask about their assessment experience
  • Discuss the career recommendations together
  • Listen to their thoughts and concerns
  • Share your own career experiences

Maintain Balance

  • Support their interests while sharing your perspective
  • Avoid imposing your career preferences
  • Focus on their strengths and passions
  • Consider practical aspects together

Encourage Exploration

  • Support them in trying new activities
  • Help arrange informational interviews
  • Visit workplaces or career fairs together
  • Encourage participation in relevant projects

Track Progress

  • Review their learning milestones
  • Celebrate achievements and badges
  • Help them set realistic goals
  • Provide encouragement during challenges

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the platform really free?

Yes, our platform is completely free. We believe every student deserves access to quality career guidance regardless of their financial situation. There are no hidden fees or premium tiers.

How accurate are the AI recommendations?

Our AI system has a 94% accuracy rate based on follow-up studies with users. However, recommendations should be viewed as guidance, not definitive answers. We encourage students to explore multiple options.

What age group is this suitable for?

Our platform is designed for students aged 14-22, covering high school through early college years. The content and assessments are age-appropriate and aligned with developmental stages.

How much time does the assessment take?

The complete 5-stage assessment typically takes 45-60 minutes. Students can take breaks between stages and don't need to complete it all at once.

Can my child retake the assessment?

Yes, students can retake the assessment after 6 months. This allows for growth and changing interests while maintaining the validity of results.

How do I access my child's results?

Your child controls their data and can choose to share results with you. We encourage open discussion about the findings as a family activity.
